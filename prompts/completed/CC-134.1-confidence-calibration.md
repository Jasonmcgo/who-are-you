## Context

Verifying CC-134's landed code surfaced a real regression in the confidence
logic (`lib/jungianStack.ts › aggregateLensStack`, ~L382–422): `high` is granted
whenever the dominant clears the in-pool runner-up by `TOP_PICK_CONVERGENCE_MARGIN
= 2` top-picks, with **no minimum-observation floor**. So a thin fixture with
**2 top-picks vs 0** clears the flat margin and registers **high**. That is why
CC-134's "?/low → ENFJ/ENTJ **high**" fixtures appeared — they are **2-vs-0
thin-data false-highs**, not confident resolutions. This is the inverse of the
problem CC-134 set out to fix, and it is **most dangerous for the population
CC-134 targets**: a respondent who leaves Q-T blocks untouched produces thin
top-pick data → a near-random leader → stamped `high`.

Symmetrically, the flat margin=2 dropped Jason's **full-data INTJ to low** — so
a flat absolute margin is simultaneously too loose on thin data and too strict
on full data. The margin should be **data-relative**, gated by a **floor**.

(Separately: the Ti+Fi co-occurrence contamination — an Fi who over-picks a
warm Ti voice — is currently unguarded on the judging axis; CC-134 §C.6 only
guards N/S. Fold the judging-axis guard in here for the legacy ranking format.)

## Tasks (all in `lib/jungianStack.ts › aggregateLensStack`)

1. **Minimum-data floor for `high` (the core fix).** Add named, owner-tunable
   constants and require, for `high`:
   - the dominant has **≥ `MIN_DOMINANT_TOP_PICKS`** top-picks (suggest 3), AND
   - the relevant pool has **≥ `MIN_QT_BLOCKS_WITH_DATA`** scored blocks
     (suggest 4 of the ~8 Q-T blocks present).
   Below either floor → `confidence = "low"` regardless of margin. (Low →
   already routes to the §D head-to-head clarifier from CC-134.)
2. **Data-relative convergence margin.** Replace the flat `TOP_PICK_CONVERGENCE_
   MARGIN = 2` with a margin that scales with observed blocks — e.g. the leader
   must clear the runner-up by `max(2, ceil(0.25 × scoredBlocks))` top-picks.
   With full 8-block data this tightens; with thin data the **floor** (Task 1)
   does the gating, not a trivially-met flat margin. Keep the constant(s) named
   + documented; the N/S valence guard (`NS_VALENCE_GUARD_MARGIN`) reuses the
   same data-relative logic.
3. **Ti+Fi / Te+Fe co-occurrence guard (judging-axis contamination).** A
   canonical stack never holds two same-attitude judging functions, so
   meaningful top-picks on **both** `Ti`&`Fi` (or both `Te`&`Fe`) is impossible
   in reality — it's the warm-Ti-pulls-Fi fingerprint. When both members of an
   impossible pair exceed a small top-pick threshold (suggest ≥2 each, or
   within the data-relative margin), force `low` and trigger the §D judging
   head-to-head clarifier. Parallel to §C.6. *(CC-138 supersedes this for binary
   sessions via the opposite-attitude constraint — note it in a comment.)*
4. **Re-validate the two failure modes.** Construct/derive:
   - a **thin** fixture (≤2 scored blocks, 2-vs-0 leader) → now `low` (was
     false-high);
   - a **full-data Jason/INTJ-shape** with a genuine dominant lead → `high`
     (no longer over-penalized by the flat margin);
   - the **Ti+Fi co-occurrence** case → `low` + clarifier.
   Show before/after confidence per affected fixture.
