Who Are You? · Body-map cards · Plate set v1

Eight cards, one body.

Single-line, monochrome ink with a quiet umber accent. The register is medical-illustration / alchemical plate, not modern UI iconography. Each card is the body part and the metaphor at once — heart and compass, antibody and flame, spine and the load it carries.

Family at a glance
Plate I

Lens · Eyes

How do you see and process reality?

An almond eye, lids deliberately rendered, iris worked as radial striations. The pupil carries fine concentric rings — attention reaching outward from inside. Closer to a botanical illustration of a flower's center than a realistic photograph.

RegisterThe gaze the user brings before the room has finished forming.
Plate II

Compass · Heart

What do you protect?

Anatomical-flavored heart outline — closer to a textbook than a valentine — overlaid with a compass rose so the heart is the compass, not heart-with-decoration. A single umber needle commits to a direction.

RegisterThe moral center that tells you where north is when something has to give.
Plate III

Conviction · Voice

What do you believe and defend?

A frontal throat — larynx, cricoid ring, tracheal rings — with three weighted vibration arcs to either side. Not a microphone, not a speech bubble. The voice represented here has cost.

RegisterThe kind of voice that doesn't speak casually.
Plate IV

Gravity · Spine

Where do cause, blame, and duty fall?

A vertebral column from C7 to sacrum, drawn as a load-bearing structure rather than a labelled diagram. A horizontal bar rests across the upper portion with weights at each end — the column under load, still upright.

RegisterThe column that stays upright when something pulls against you.
Plate V

Trust · Ears

Whom do you trust to mediate reality?

An ear in profile — helix, antihelix, tragus, lobe — receiving four concentric sound arcs from the left in umber. The ear is the channel through which truth reaches the self; the channel is selective.

RegisterReceptive, listening, discerning.
Plate VI

Weather · Nervous System

What shaped you, and what surrounds you now?

A trunk forking into progressively finer branches — a dendritic field that reads as neuron, lightning fork, or the wind-pattern of a storm front. A small umber node-cluster marks a signaling intersection at the central junction.

RegisterThe body that's been answering questions about its environment for a long time.
Plate VII

Fire · Immune Response

What survives when belief costs you something?

An antibody Y-shape inside a faint cellular outline — the immunoglobulin's stem, hinge, and two Fab arms with binding-site terminals. A small umber flame sits at the hinge: defensive activation, contained, not destruction.

RegisterThe system that comes online when a value is challenged.
Plate VIII

Path · Gait

What are you becoming?

Four footprints — alternating left-right — receding into the canvas. Each is anatomically flavored (heel, ball, toes) but rendered as a beach-sand impression in clean line work. The fourth is faint, on the way to something not yet visible.

RegisterThe rhythm of a life moving through its days.
